# Audience: Ukrainian Doctors (Сімейні лікарі)

**Product:** AI-помічник лікаря (digital toolkit, €19/799₴)
**Last updated:** 2026-02-22
**Status:** validated (market research v3 complete, product live)

---

## Who they are

- **Core:** practicing doctors, family medicine (сімейні лікарі)
- **Gender:** ~80% women
- **Age:** average ~50 years old (range 35-60)
- **Geography:** Ukraine (Phase 1), CIS without Russia (Phase 2: KZ, GE, AM, MD)
- **Salary:** ~23,800₴/month ($580). 799₴ = 3.4% of monthly salary — NOT an impulse buy for them
- **Work context:** 70% burnout rate, 83% report increased stress due to war
- **AI adoption:** 66% already use AI (was 38% in 2023, +78% in one year). 81% want more training
- **Where they work:** public & private clinics, hospitals, FOP private practices

## Pains & fears

### Professional pains
- Drowning in paperwork — documentation eats 30-40% of working time
- Burnout — emotional and administrative exhaustion
- Outdated knowledge — hard to keep up with new guidelines/protocols
- War-related stress — patients with PTSD, supply issues, constant uncertainty

### Fears (what keeps them up at night)
1. **Fear of falling behind colleagues** — "they already use AI, I don't" (FOMO 8/10)
2. **Fear of making mistakes with AI** — they think they know how to use ChatGPT, but don't understand hallucinations, limitations, or safety risks
3. **Fear of technology** — "this is too technical for me" (biggest purchase barrier)
4. **Fear of irrelevance** — "doctors who use AI will replace doctors who don't" (most powerful messaging lever)
5. **War-era anxiety** — general survival mode, uncertainty about future

### What happens if they DON'T solve it
- Spend 2+ extra hours daily on documentation
- Fall behind colleagues who automate
- Miss new guidelines, risk outdated treatment decisions
- Burnout → leave medicine

## What they pay for & how much

- **Currently buying:** CME/NMO courses (mandatory), medical conferences, Medvoice/MedІвент subscriptions
- **Price sensitivity:** HIGH. 23,800₴ salary. Carefully evaluate every purchase
- **799₴ context:** = 2 lunches out. Cheap relative to courses (10-25K₴), but NOT "don't even think about it" cheap for this salary
- **What competitors charge:**
  - UA courses: Hillel 11,610₴, WebPromoExperts 22,500₴, Franch.AI €199-1000
  - Global: Harvard $2,500, ABAIM $500-1500, Stanford $49/month
  - Generic prompt packs: $3-15 on Gumroad (race to bottom, low sales)
  - **Gap:** no medical AI toolkit at €19 in Ukrainian/Russian

## How they make decisions

- **Where they look for info:** Facebook groups ("Доказові батьки" ~400K), MedІвент, Medvoice, GMKA, colleagues. NOT Telegram (TG channels for doctors barely exist in UA)
- **Who influences:** colleagues, department heads, medical opinion leaders. Evidence-based mindset — they want data, not hype
- **Purchase triggers:**
  - Colleague recommends or demonstrates a result
  - Seeing concrete before/after ("discharge note: 40 min → 5 min")
  - Professional certification/CME credits attached
  - Social proof from other doctors (not from tech people)
- **Barriers to buying:**
  1. "Too technical for me" — #1 blocker. Must show "no coding required"
  2. "These are just prompts, I can Google them myself" — must sell WORKFLOWS, not prompts
  3. "I need a paid AI subscription" — must highlight "works with free AI tools"
  4. "AI is hype, won't work in a year" — must show evidence-based positioning
  5. "Won't work for my specialty" — must show diverse use cases across specialties

## Specifics & nuances

- **Technical literacy: LOW.** Average age ~50. Many don't know what Notion is. PDF = zero friction. Notion = barrier. Mobile-first (often use phone between patients)
- **Language:** Ukrainian. "AI" → "ШІ" (штучний інтелект) in all client-facing text. "Workflow" → "готове рішення". "Template/шаблон" → also confusing, "рішення" works better
- **Evidence-based mindset:** doctors are trained to evaluate evidence. Generic marketing claims = instant distrust. MUST use specific numbers with sources (e.g., "66% лікарів — AMA, 2024")
- **Visual preferences:** clean, professional, light backgrounds. Tech/node/space aesthetics = anti-pattern. Competitors (Stanford, Harvard) use white space + human photos
- **Trust signals that work:** university logos/seals, professional certifications, peer testimonials with real names, specific statistics with citations
- **Trust signals that DON'T work:** generic "5000+ users", tech buzzwords, startup aesthetics, stock photos
- **"Already using AI" ≠ "using it well."** Even experienced ChatGPT users among doctors don't know about hallucinations, RAG, or AI limitations. They think they know because ChatGPT always sounds confident. This = positioning opportunity: "protection from AI mistakes" works for beginners AND current users
- **Time is everything.** Most powerful concrete benefit: "come home 2 hours earlier." Abstract "less paperwork" doesn't land
- **Certificate matters.** Doctors want a piece of paper for their CV. Even for a €19 product, a verifiable certificate (QR code) adds perceived value
- **26% of @medicalforua audience already asks ChatGPT instead of Google.** Marketing angle: "your patients already ask ChatGPT instead of you"

## Competitors for this audience

- **In Ukraine:** ZERO specialized AI courses/products for doctors in Ukrainian

- **What they do differently:** short videos <6min, certification, CME credits, hands-on labs
